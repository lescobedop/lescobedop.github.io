> **道を広くしれば、物毎に出会ふこと也。**

---

There is a quality that shows up the same way regardless of the terrain. I have felt it on the mat and I have felt it in a room full of people I was supposed to convince of something. The same steadiness. The same particular form of attention. Whatever I was doing when I first developed it, that is not where it stays.

Miyamoto Musashi wrote it plainly: know the Way broadly and you will find it in all things. Not mastery of a single discipline, but the mastery of a way of moving through the unfamiliar. The point was never the sword.

Discomfort is the constant variable. Every new terrain introduces it. A language you do not yet know. A discipline whose vocabulary is unfamiliar. A city that has its own unwritten rules you have not been given. The temptation is to wait until the discomfort subsides before proceeding, as if clarity is a precondition for action. It is not. The clarity comes from proceeding.

The still patience that steadied my hands in one place has steadied my thinking in another. The attentiveness that served under one kind of pressure finds its footing under a different kind. Whatever the quality is, it transfers. The only requirement is that you recognize it when it appears in a new form, and trust it enough to act.

The unfamiliar does not remain so. You learn its logic, its rhythm, the way it rewards and the way it punishes. And then it begins to feel like all the other places you have learned. Not the same, but subject to the same patience.

The Way is not in any single place. It is in how you meet what you do not know yet.
